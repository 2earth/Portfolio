University of Bath
I have just finished the 4th of 5 years in my integrated masters degree (with year-long placement) in Integrated Design Engineering (MEng) at the University of Bath.
Rear row, 4th from left.

Having spent the first two years studying general mechanical engineering, I made the decision to specialise in Integrated Design Engineering. This was primarily because I wanted a program that focussed more on practical project work, allowing greater creativity and requiring a broad set of skills, including programming, electronics and problem solving. I'm now in a group of 16 students taking the course.
