CAD Modelling
To support university and placement projects I have used a variety of CAD modelling softwares; Siemens Solid Edge ST6 and ST8, Catia, Autodesk Inventor and Fusion 360.
While working at MG I produced a CAD model of a prototype seat using Catia V5.
In the Body department I modelled an A-post baffle to which the 'shotgun' panel was mounted using Catia V5.
In my group business and design project, I designed and modelled a range of mechatronic attachments for the Dyson 360 eye to teach secondary students computer science. Modelled using Autodesk Inventor.
The grippers were made from as few lasercut pieced of MDF as possible.
The launcher's dimensions needed to be very specific to allow the trigger to function correctly.
The robot arm needed to be sturdy but also easy to assemble.
At the start of my second year I worked for three weeks on a User-Centred Design Project. As part of this, I modelled an ergonomic pivoting coin-return mechanism for 3D printing using Autodesk Fusion 360.
In my first year I designed a snowmobile transmission shaft using simple FEA calculations, selecting bearings and pulleys based on lifetime duty requirements. Modelled using Siemens Solid Edge ST6.
In my second year I worked with another student to design a conveyor system which could fold jeans prior to shipping. Modelled using Siemens Solid Edge ST8
In another two-person design project, I designed a human-powered washing machine. Modelled using Siemens Solid Edge ST8.
In my first year I worked in a team of 6 to design a table tennis ball launcher, which would fire 6 balls across the width of a table. I produced the CAD assembly using Siemens Solid Edge ST6.
