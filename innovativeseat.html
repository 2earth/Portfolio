While working in the trim department at MG I was asked to generate ideas that would challenge the dominant automotive seating design, featuring two linear rails to adjust forward positioning. First, I brainstormed concepts.
I presented each concept to my team who each ranked the concepts against agreed criteria. I then used Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) to evaluate the most promising direction.
I then developed the chosen concept, which featured one rail mounted above the transmission tunnel and one under a false floor. A CAD model was produced to support a detailed design.
Following this I supplied designer Paolo Liuzzi with my CAD model, who produced a series of professional concept drawings to support my design.
I then presented my design progress to the head of Vehicle Engineering, who awarded budget to the project to support a prototype. Further detailed design supported by my seating colleagues, followed by procurement, led to a building phase.
I manufactured as much of the final prototype as possible, following my own engineering drawings. The seat could be locked or slid forward after pulling a lever as per the standard design. However, the seat rails were hidden from view behind tambour in the transmission tunnel and under a false floor. This could allow improved ergonomics or storage possiblities.
