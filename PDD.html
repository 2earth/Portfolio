Sustainable Toys
A third year university project was to design a product which would address the brief of sustainable toys posed by the RSA. The purpose of the project was also to place a focus on the design tools and methodology utilised. Working in a group of four, I led the final stage of the design, converging the concept space, drawing the concepts and selecting a final concept.
This was done using Pugh's controlled convergence method, which allowed a concept addition phase after each round of selection.
Concepts were ranked as better, the same as or worse than the datum design.
My concept the fantasy kit responded to cognitive development research by allowing young children to create their own "fantasy" environment in which to play socially in an unprescripted manner.
My second concept, the craft kit aimed to facilitate childrens' natural creativity, using "found" or upcycled objects when possible. The space would live and develop with the child.

The robot arm needed to be sturdy but also easy to assemble.
The immersive fantasy concept used auditory prompts to loosely guide children through a game scenario, suggesting they use household objects to improvise elements of the game.
The final concept fantasy natura integrated the strengths of different ideas into an immersive projected environment which improvised elements from household and found objects. Shown here, children play together to defend their ship from sharks on their way to dig for pirate treasure. This addressed the brief by replacing ephemeral plastic toys with an exciting play environment which would develop childrens' social and creative skills.
